Final Client / Developer Project Report

Client Report

For my personal website, I chose content that reflected who I am, what I am studying, and my future goals in cybersecurity. I included information about my academic journey, personal interests, and my upcoming internship in Japan because it is an important part of my future plans. I also selected images that matched the theme of the website and helped make it feel more personal.

I communicated mostly with my professor throughout the project since I did not have a traditional development team. Communication was helpful overall, and I was able to receive feedback and make updates when needed.

The final site met my expectations and represented the vision I had for my website.

If I were to do this again, I would spend more time planning the layout and organization of the website before starting.

Project Development Experience

Because I did not work with a normal team structure, my experience was more independent and focused on working closely with my professor for guidance and feedback. This helped me better understand how to communicate ideas clearly and apply feedback to improve the project.

One of the biggest learning experiences for me was using GitHub and GitHub Pages. At first it was confusing, especially when it came to publishing the site and updating changes correctly, but over time I became much more comfortable with it.

Working with my professor as my partner made the experience feel more like a real-world project because I had to communicate professionally and stay aware of deadlines and expectations.

General Experience and Reflections

The most challenging part of this project for me was understanding some of the instructions and figuring out exactly what was expected for each part of the assignment. I was often confused on certain sections, which caused me to fall behind and be late on parts of the project.

Once I asked questions and got clarification, it became much easier to move forward and complete the work. This taught me the importance of asking for help sooner instead of waiting until I was already behind.

What I enjoyed most was seeing the final website come together and being able to create something that felt personal to me and connected to my future goals.

If I could do the project again, I would start earlier, ask questions sooner, and make sure I fully understood each step before moving on.

This experience made me feel more confident in working on future real-world projects because I learned not only coding skills, but also the importance of communication, time management, and asking for clarification.
